Dudley MakerSpace

A community mental health demonstration project


Project Summary

Dudley MakerSpace is a community mental health demonstration project that provides a safe and supportive environment for individuals to explore their creativity and improve their mental well-being. The MakerSpace offers a variety of tools, resources, and workshops to help participants develop new skills, connect with others, and find joy in making.


Objectives
  • To create a welcoming and inclusive space for individuals of all backgrounds to engage in creative activities.
  • To provide access to tools and resources that support mental health and well-being.
  • To foster a sense of community and connection among participants.
  • To promote the benefits of creativity and making for mental health.
  • To establish a space for community mental health rehabilitation and support.
Activities and Workshops

Dudley MakerSpace offers a range of activities and workshops designed to support mental health and well-being. These include:

  • Workshops on mindfulness and stress management.
  • Workshops on self-expression through art and crafts.
  • Group projects that encourage collaboration and social interaction.
  • Access to tools and resources for DIY projects and creative endeavors.
  • Specialist talks and presentations from mental health professionals.
Impact and Outcomes

Dudley Makerspace operated for 21 months from April 2018 to December 2019 on Trindle Road, Dudley

  • 28 people joined the MakerSpace with four core committed volunteers
  • Volunteers regularly took part in organizing events and supporting participants
  • We engaged with a number of governmental and non-governmental organizations to enhance our services and reach a wider audience
  • The confusion and difficulty of creating a meaningful offer in a limited space ultimately led to the project's conclusion.
